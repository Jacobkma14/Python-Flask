📊 Project: Case Study 1 – Daily Activity Analysis

📋 Project Description

As a Business Analytics student at Cambrian College, this project aimed to evaluate daily habits between September 9, 2024, to February 23, 2025. Using personal data, I analyzed patterns around study hours, sleep, happiness, and other personal variables to understand time management and balance in student life.

📌 Key Variables

  • Study Hours, Sleep, and Pages Read – Quantitative measures of daily activity
  • Color of Clothing, Class, and Watch News – Categorical variables for personal and academic habits
  • Happiness Level – Ordinal variable rated from 1 to 5

📈 Statistical Summary

Sleep: Average = 7.17 hrs (SD = 1.1)

Study: Average = 3.7 hrs (SD = 1.24)

Pages Read: Average = 4.58 pages (SD = 1.55)

📊 Hypothesis Tests

  • Study Hours > 3.13: p-value < 0.001 – Sufficient evidence to reject null.
  • Sleep ≠ 7 hrs: p-value = 0.0703 – Fail to reject null, avg sleep ≈ 7 hrs.
  • Pages Read ≠ 5: p-value = 0.0016 – Strong evidence mean pages read ≠ 5.

📚 Semester Comparison

First semester study hours (Mean = 3.86) were higher than second semester (Mean = 3.38), supported by t-test p-value = 0.0118.

Paired t-tests on 10-day samples found no significant difference in study hours.

📌 Conclusion

The report reflects consistent study and sleep patterns with scope to increase reading habits. This case study helps in better time allocation and emphasizes the importance of self-reflection using data analytics techniques.
